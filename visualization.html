YOUNG PEOPLE DURING THIS PANDEMIC

The future seems uncertain.

Introduction

For many recent college graduates, the coronavirus pandemic has impacted their career plans and their financial outlook "This whole pandemic has made many young people concerned and confused about what their future looks like," Now that the economy has taken a hit, young adults will need to make some adjustments. Here are some things they can do to land a job and create some financial stability.

Get creative

Thinking creatively

This is also a good time to think creatively when it comes to your career, It's making us think a little bit more critically." It is not about giving up on their passion. For example a youth who was doing photography as his/her career could maybe take extra photo editing jobs for money right now, or even give photography tutorials on her own social media pages.

live like a broke college student

Times like these call for reining in spending Most youths will move to live at home with their parents longer to save money. They might also concentrate on staying out of debts or paying off the debt they have. At the moment. This will help them in building good financial habits.

Ask for assistance

Guidance is available to those who need help. For instance, counselors and coaches who are certified are offering free virtual sessions. Which is really good resource for people who are really in a hard financial spot and need a little bit of counseling.

Things they can do:

Discoveries

•Try something you’ve never done. With so many how-to videos on YouTube, learn a new skill like yoga, designing clothes or knitting to give you a creative release.

Volunteer

Find opportunities to volunteer in your community. If there are older people or immunocompromised people help them out. Help out vulnerable people in your community to pick up their groceries and medicine.
